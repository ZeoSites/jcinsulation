Insulation Types

Spray Foam Insulation

Spray foam insulation is widely known as the best and most durable insulation type out there. It has the ability to create both effective thermal and vapor barricades. It comes in the form of liquid, but on application, it expands and transforms into foam.

It has the ability to create impressive sealing properties that is nearly impossible for the other types of insulation to form. Spray foam insulation is much preferred over the other types of insulations because of its many advantages such as durability, ease of application, effortless care, and it is environmentally friendly.

But it is important to note that this type of insulation can only function to its full potential with proper installation. And that is why it is important to always call on professionals for your insulation projects.

Cellulose Insulation

Insulation made from recycled materials is made up of mostly recycled materials such as newspaper, paper fiber, denim, wood, and so on. This type of insulation is one of the oldest insulation types, and it is still being used till date. People often go for this type of insulation because it is green, able to resist flames, easy to install, and it can easily penetrate every nook and crannies intended for it to fill up.

Nevertheless, Cellulose insulation has its downside too, for example, it can easily lose its ability to maintain energy efficiency, it can enhance the production of mold growth, fungal growth, and so on because it is porous to water and it can retain moisture.

Fiberglass Insulation

Fiberglass is a type of insulation material made mostly from glass. People usually go for this type of insulation because it is fast and easy to install, the cost is inexpensive, It is versatile, helpful in saving energy, and also green.

However, fiberglass also has its downside which might not make it the number one choice for most homeowners and insulation professionals as well.

Some of its downside includes lack of long term durability unlike foam spray insulation, lack of adaptability that can make it fit into any shape or space and seal it up properly, likelihood of mold formation and its likes because of its porosity to water.
